THE UNIVERSITY OF MANCHESTER

The University of Manchester is the largest single-site university in the United Kingdom, boasting the largest student community as well. As part of the Russell Group, the University produces vast ammounts of top-level research, with the discovery of graphene being the most notable recent event.
With 25 Nobel Prize winners having worked or studied here, and with over 90% of students going straight into employment or continuing their studies, the University of Manchester offers huge prospects for students and staff alike. Read more >>

SCHOOL OF COMPUTER SCIENCE

The School of Computer Science at the University of Manchester is one of the oldest schools in the UK. The University of Manchester has made significant contributions to the field of computer science. This includes many innovations such as the creation of the first stored-program computer (the Baby), the first transistor computer, the first floating point machine, and the first computer to use virtual memory.
