The University of Manchester was formed in 2004 when University of Manchester Institute of Science and Technology and the Victoria University of Manchester merged. It is part of the Russell Group universities with a wide selection of courses and outstanding facilities. The University of Manchester has a main campus which is located on Oxford Road (south of Manchester City Center) and has a north campus centered on Sackville Street. The University has approximately 40,000 students making it the largest single-site university in the United Kingdom.

The University of Manchester consists of three main faculties as of August 2016. These include the Faculty of Biology Medicine and Health, the Faculty of Science and Engineering and the Faculty of Humanities. Each faculty is then subdivided into many schools. The Faculty of Science and Engineering includes the schools of Computer Science, Chemistry, Material Science, Mathematics, Physics and Astronomy, Earth and Environmental Science, MACE (Mechanical, Aerospace and Civil Engineering), EEE (Electrical and Electronic Engineering), Chemical Engineering and Civil Engineering.
