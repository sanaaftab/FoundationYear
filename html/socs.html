The School of Computer Science at the University of Manchester is the oldest and also one of the largest schools of computer science established in the United Kingdom. The school is located in the Kilburn Building on Oxford Road. There are currently around 800 undergraduate and postgraduate students at the School of Computer Science, and about 60 full-time academic staff.

The University of Manchester has made significant contributions to the field of computer science. This includes many innovations such as the creation of the Manchester Small-Scale Experimental Machine (SSEM), nicknamed the Baby, which was the first stored-program computer. The University is also credited with making the first transistor computer, the first floating point machine, and the first computer to use virtual memory.
